Top Sci-Fi Movies of the 80's & 90's

Top Five Picks

Bladerunner

Released in 1982. 'Bladerunner' is an icon of the Cyberpunk genre. It serves as a cornertone of classic futuristic dystopia and a question mark on the topics of Mega Corporations, Cybernetics, Cloning and Human Consiousness, etc..

The Running Man

A film released in 1987. 'The Running Man' is a film that follows in the era of classical dystopia, with it's basis founded on the monetization of the penitentiary system by the entertainment industry through galdiatory shows.

Terminator

A 1984 sci-fi icon. 'Terminator' continues to serve as a reference to rouge Artificial Intelligence, killer robots and the effects of time travel.

Terminator 2: Judgement Day

A 1991 continuation of the Terminator icon. 'Terminator 2:Judgement Day' leans heavily into themes of time travel and the philosophical question of wether Artificial Intelligence is able to empathize.

2001: A Space Odyssey

A 1986 icon. '2001: A Space Odyssey', serves as a first in the use of wide open scenes and minor dialouge. The film serves as an basis in which man and machine must reconcile there differences on the path to space exploration and discovery.
